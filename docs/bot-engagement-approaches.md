# GharJi WhatsApp Assistant — Engagement Approaches

**Prepared for:** GharJi
**Date:** July 2026
**Subject:** How the WhatsApp assistant should engage people who message your business number

---

## Overview

The GharJi WhatsApp assistant collects property listings through a guided,
tap-to-answer conversation (owner/broker, rent/sale, property type, location,
price, size, photos, contact). The listing conversation itself is the same in
every case — the question this document answers is **when the assistant should
step in, and when your team should own the conversation.**

We evaluated three approaches. All three have been built and tested; our
recommendation is Approach 3.

---

## Approach 1 — Always On

**How it works:** The assistant responds to every message. Anyone who texts
the number — for any reason — is immediately taken into the listing
questionnaire.

| Strengths | Limitations |
|---|---|
| Zero friction: property owners are captured instantly, no instructions needed | The assistant answers *every* message — your team can never chat manually on this number |
| Simplest possible experience for genuine listers | Someone asking a simple question (e.g. office hours) is met with "Are you the Owner or a Broker?" |
| | Every listing enquiry is treated the same as casual or unrelated messages |

**Best suited for:** a dedicated, listings-only number promoted purely for
property submissions — not a general business line.

---

## Approach 2 — On Demand (Keyword Activated)

**How it works:** The assistant stays completely silent until someone types
the word **"list"**. That keyword starts the listing conversation; every other
message is left untouched for your team to answer personally.

| Strengths | Limitations |
|---|---|
| Your team fully owns the number — the assistant never interrupts a human conversation | New customers don't know the keyword — a first-time "Hi, I want to sell my flat" gets **no reply at all**, which looks like the number is ignored |
| Works beautifully with marketing links: a "List your property" button on your website or ads can open WhatsApp with "list" pre-filled, so customers never type it | Depends entirely on marketing to teach people the keyword |
| The keyword also acts as a reset — typing "list" anytime restarts a stuck conversation | Walk-in / word-of-mouth customers fall through the cracks |

**Best suited for:** businesses whose WhatsApp traffic arrives mainly through
website buttons, ads, or QR codes that pre-fill the keyword.

---

## Approach 3 — Greet Once, Then Hand Over ⭐ *Recommended*

**How it works:** The **first time** a number ever messages you, the assistant
sends a single welcome with three tappable options:

> **Welcome to GharJi 🏡** — How can I help you today?
> &nbsp;&nbsp;▸ **List a property** — starts the guided listing conversation
> &nbsp;&nbsp;▸ **Enquire** — captures what they're looking for; your team follows up with options
> &nbsp;&nbsp;▸ **Talk to our team** — the assistant steps aside immediately

After that one welcome, the assistant goes quiet for that person. Your team
owns the chat. Typing **"list"** summons the assistant again at any time, so
the Approach 2 keyword (and its marketing-link trick) still works.

| Strengths | Limitations |
|---|---|
| No customer is ever met with silence — everyone gets a professional welcome and a clear path | Requires the system to remember who has already been welcomed, so it depends on the permanent database (planned next step) |
| No conversation is ever hijacked — the assistant interrupts each person exactly once | The "Enquire" option currently hands off to your team; a fully guided buyer search is a planned future addition |
| Every visitor is sorted into a lead type from their very first message: lister, buyer enquiry, or general | |
| Keeps the "list" keyword, so marketing links keep working | |

**Best suited for:** a real business number that serves everyone — listers,
buyers, and general customers — which is GharJi's situation.

---

## Side-by-Side Summary

| | Approach 1 — Always On | Approach 2 — On Demand | Approach 3 — Greet Once ⭐ |
|---|---|---|---|
| New customer gets an immediate response | ✅ | ❌ (silence) | ✅ |
| Your team can chat manually on the number | ❌ | ✅ | ✅ |
| Buyers / general enquiries handled gracefully | ❌ | ➖ (by team only) | ✅ (sorted at the door) |
| Works with pre-filled marketing links | n/a | ✅ | ✅ |
| Ready today | ✅ | ✅ | ✅ (full reliability arrives with the database step) |

---

## Recommendation

**Adopt Approach 3.** It combines the strengths of the other two: every
customer gets an instant, professional first impression, yet your team keeps
full control of every human conversation. It also lays the groundwork for the
next phase — buyer search — since the "Enquire" door is already in place.

**Next step:** connect the assistant to its permanent database so it reliably
remembers conversations and welcomed customers. This is a small, already-scoped
task and is the final item before the assistant is production-ready.
